## 1. Product Vision

AI Stock Analyst aims to become a beginner-friendly AI investment research assistant that helps users understand the stock market, analyze their holdings, and make more informed investment decisions.

The product is designed for people who are interested in investing but may not have the time, knowledge, or focus to manually read financial news, market charts, macroeconomic updates, and company reports every day.

Instead of overwhelming users with complex financial dashboards, the application should translate market data and news into simple, practical, and AI-generated explanations.

The long-term vision is to build a low-barrier investment research platform that can gradually evolve into a more professional market analysis system.

## 2. Target Vision

The first version is mainly for personal use and close users such as friends, family members, or beginner investors.

The target users may include:

- People who know little about investing
- People who trade or invest based on feeling
- People who want to invest but do not want to study the market deeply
- People who want to grow their money or beat inflation
- People who want AI to help them understand market movement
- People who want simple suggestions instead of complex financial analysis

The application should help these users move from emotional or random investing toward more structured, informed, and explainable investment decisions.

## 3. Product Direction

The product should start as an AI investment assistant rather than a professional trading terminal.

In the MVP, the product should feel more like an **AI investment secretary**:

- It explains what happened in the market
- It summarizes important news
- It checks the user's holdings
- It gives simple investment-oriented suggestions
- It explains the reasoning behind each suggestion
- It helps the user decide what to research further

In the long term, the product may gradually add more professional features and become closer to a simplified Bloomberg-style research platform.

Future versions may support different user modes:

1. **Beginner Mode**
   - Simple explanations
   - Clear action suggestions
   - Low financial jargon
   - Focus on understanding and decision support

2. **Professional Mode**
   - More charts
   - More financial metrics
   - More technical indicators
   - Deeper company analysis
   - More customizable panels

The MVP should focus on Beginner Mode.

## 4. Platform Vision

The ideal product form is a web application.

The first version should be simple to run locally. It should not require extra work for mobile app or desktop app adaptation.

The product may later evolve into a deployed website with a domain name.

Possible future platform directions include:

- Public web app
- User account system
- Subscription-based features
- Report generation service
- Premium analysis modules
- Email report delivery
- More advanced user profile and portfolio management

The first version will not include email reports or subscription features.

## 5. User Account Vision

The product should eventually support user accounts.

Each user should be able to store personal investment data in the database, such as:

- Holdings
- Watchlist
- Investment preferences
- Risk tolerance
- Investment time horizon
- Past reports
- Company notes
- Investment thesis

For the MVP, the system only needs basic database support. The first version does not need a complex onboarding flow.

In future versions, the product may include a guided onboarding experience similar to financial apps or banking apps. The onboarding flow could help users define:

- Investment goals
- Risk tolerance
- Preferred sectors
- Long-term vs short-term preference
- Income vs growth preference
- Current investing experience
- Portfolio size range
- Preferred report style

This would allow the AI system to generate more personalized analysis.

## 6. Language Vision

The MVP will support English only.

All reports, UI text, prompts, and AI-generated analysis should be written in English.

Chinese or bilingual support may be considered in future versions, but it is not part of the first version.

## 7. Accessibility Vision

The product should be easy for beginners to understand.

The system should avoid unnecessary financial jargon. When financial terms are needed, the AI should explain them clearly.

The user should not need to understand professional market analysis tools in order to use the product.

The ideal experience is:

1. The user opens the web app.
2. The app loads the user's holdings and watchlist.
3. The app fetches market and news data.
4. The AI generates a structured report.
5. The user reads a clear summary and suggested actions.
6. The user can ask follow-up questions if needed.

## 8. Long-Term Product Identity

AI Stock Analyst should not be positioned as a tool that guarantees profit.

It should be positioned as:

**A beginner-friendly AI investment research assistant that helps users understand the market, analyze holdings, and make more informed investment decisions.**

The product should help users become less emotional and more structured in how they invest.

## 9. Future Expansion

Future versions may include:

- Public website deployment
- User authentication
- Personalized portfolio storage
- Beginner investment onboarding
- Investment style profiling
- Professional analysis mode
- Sector-specific dashboards
- AI-generated watchlist recommendations
- Technical analysis panels
- Earnings analysis
- Valuation support
- Subscription-based report generation
- Email or notification system
- Family or beginner investor mode
- More advanced portfolio review
- Risk exposure analysis
